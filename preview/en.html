Selected Projects

Energy Utility · Regulatory Reporting · 2017-2025

Building a Regulatory Reporting Platform from Scratch

Platform Architecture End-to-End Technical Ownership Regulatory Reporting

The Challenge

Regulatory reporting obligations for electricity, heat, and gas consumption were largely manual, requiring significant personnel effort and causing high operational costs. The existing process focused solely on generating mandatory reports for authorities, without enabling any downstream business use cases from the collected data. As the number of metering devices grew, the approach was no longer economically sustainable.

The Intervention

As Tech Lead with end-to-end responsibility, I designed the overall platform architecture and guided its implementation. I defined clear system boundaries across ingestion, normalization, aggregation, and reporting, ensuring independent scalability and long-term extensibility.

The Outcome

Manual reporting workflows were eliminated and replaced by automated, on-demand generation of authority-facing regulatory reports. The platform established a scalable data foundation that fulfills reporting obligations while enabling additional business and analytics use cases beyond mandatory reporting.

Stack

Spring Boot · Kubernetes · MQTT · Neo4j · Google Cloud Pub/Sub · Docker · OpenAPI · React

Energy / Data · 2023-2024

Enabling a Strategic Database Decision Under Delivery Pressure

Architecture Decision-Making Migration Risk Reduction Graph Data Modeling

The Challenge

A platform migration required replacing an existing ArangoDB-based solution, which was no longer viable from a cost and complexity perspective. The strategic decision was whether to adopt a graph-native database (Neo4j) or build a more complex alternative. The decision had to be validated quickly to avoid delivery delays.

The Intervention

I assessed architectural options under delivery pressure by designing and implementing a focused proof of concept for the platform’s core use cases. I supported the team by translating existing data access logic into a production-ready graph-based model.

The Outcome

The proof of concept enabled a fast and confident architectural decision. Migration risk was significantly reduced, allowing the platform transition to be completed within the committed timeline without unnecessary complexity.

Stack

Neo4j · Spring Boot · Cypher · ArangoDB · JSON Schema · JUnit

Telecommunications · pre-cloud era

Replacing a Costly Legacy CMS Without a Full Rewrite

Legacy Modernization Strategy Cost & Risk Reduction Large-scale System Design

The Challenge

A legacy CMS caused high and growing operational costs and had to be decommissioned. The proposed full rewrite exceeded timeline and budget constraints and introduced additional licensing risk, putting the project’s success at risk.

The Intervention

I challenged the rewrite-based approach and designed an alternative solution that preserved existing business logic. By enabling legacy templates to run in a modern application environment, I removed the dependency on the original runtime.

The Outcome

A full rewrite was avoided, significantly reducing migration effort and cost. The legacy system was decommissioned on time and within budget, without introducing new vendor dependencies.
